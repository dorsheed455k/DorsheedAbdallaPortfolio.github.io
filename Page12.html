Bandpass Filter Course Project

The objective of this project was to design and realize a manufacturable microstrip bandpass filter that meets specified return loss and insertion loss requirements using a complete RF design workflow. Classical filter synthesis techniques are applied to translate frequency-domain specifications into physical microstrip implementations, including coupled-line and quarter-wave resonator topologies on a Rogers RO4003C substrate. The design is developed and tuned using Keysight ADS schematic simulations and validated through ADS Momentum electromagnetic analysis to capture parasitic and distributed effects. Manufacturing constraints such as minimum trace dimensions and substrate stack-up are enforced during layout optimization, and schematic-to-EM comparisons are used to evaluate performance degradation due to physical implementation, resulting in a fabrication-ready PCB design that reflects real-world RF design trade-offs.
