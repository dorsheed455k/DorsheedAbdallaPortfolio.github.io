Multi-Source Inverter Capstone Project

As part of our capstone, my group is tasked with designing a novel multi-source inverter that incorporates FOC functionality in order to control a 60W BLDC motor connected through 3-phase inputs. It is composed of three major sections, starting with the multi-source switching circuitry, followed by the 2-level inverter topology, and then the digital control section. The project utilizes the STM32G491VET6 MCU specialized for motor control applications, along with other peripheral devices such as a hall-speed sensor, Bluetooth module, and galvanically isolated gate drivers and current/voltage sensors for feedback. Tools and software used throughout the course of this project include PSIM for power electronics simulation, Altium Designer for PCB design, Ansys Electronics for PCB Thermal/EMC simulations, Solidworks for system modelling and STM32CubeIDE for firmware development.
